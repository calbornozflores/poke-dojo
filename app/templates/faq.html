Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Poke-Dojo.

What is Poke-Dojo?

Poke-Dojo is a local Pokémon quiz game covering all 1025 Pokémon. It features five Dojo training modes, a Battle Arena with VS Mode and Solo Challenge, and a Daily Challenge — all with score tracking, a personal profile with AI analysis, and a Trainer Journey chart.

What game modes are available?

Dojo (5 modes):
· Name It — Easy: full artwork shown, type the Pokémon name (fuzzy matching)
· Name It — Medium: same as Easy but stricter scoring
· Name It — Hard: silhouette only — identify from the outline
· Guess the Number: name + artwork shown, enter the Pokédex number
· Guess the Type — Easy: pick primary type from 4 choices
· Guess the Type — Hard: toggle all correct types from 18 options

Battle Arena:
· VS Mode: 2 players, same keyboard — silhouette reveals over 10 seconds
· Solo Challenge: endless rounds, 3 lives, tightening timer — your AI Shadow competes against you
· Daily Challenge: Wordle-style — guess today's mystery Pokémon

What is the EVO score?

The EVO score is an exponential moving average of your final scores across all game sessions. It rises as you improve and is charted over time on the Trainer Journey page, giving you a single number that reflects your overall performance trend.

What is the Professor Oak Analysis?

After completing 20 games in a mode, the Professor Oak Analysis unlocks on your Profile page. An XGBoost model trained on your answers uses SHAP values to identify which Pokémon categories (by type, generation, or evolution stage) you find hardest. The app then preferentially selects those Pokémon for you 80% of the time, so you train where you need it most.

How does the Daily Challenge work?

Every day a new mystery Pokémon is chosen — the same one for all players worldwide. Type any Pokémon name into the autocomplete input and press Enter. After each guess you see a distance score:

· Distance = name similarity (50%) + Pokédex proximity (50%)
· 0.000 = exact match · 1.000 = as far as possible

🟩 ≤ 0.2 — Very close   🟨 0.2–0.5 — Warmer   🟥 > 0.5 — Far away

Guesses are sorted closest-first. The Pokémon resets at midnight each day.

How does sign-in with Google work?

Signing in with Google lets you claim a permanent trainer nickname that appears on the global leaderboard and Battle Arena rankings. You choose your own nickname — your Google email is never displayed or shared anywhere in the app.

Is my data private?

Game results are stored in a local SQLite database on the server. Your chosen trainer nickname is public on the leaderboard — that is the only piece of information other players can see. No email address, real name, or Google account details are ever displayed or accessible to other users.

I found a bug — what do I do?

Please open an issue on GitHub: github.com/calbornozflores/poke-dojo/issues . Describe what you expected, what happened, and any steps to reproduce it. You can also reach out via the Contact page.
